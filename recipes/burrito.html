Burrito

Description

dish in Mexican[2] and Tex-Mex cuisine[3] that took form in California cuisine, consisting of a flour tortilla wrapped into a sealed cylindrical shape around various ingredients. The tortilla is sometimes lightly grilled or steamed to soften it, make it more pliable, and allow it to adhere to itself. Burritos are often eaten by hand, as their tight wrapping keeps the ingredients together. Burritos can also be served "wet", i.e., covered in a savory and spicy sauce, when they would be eaten with a fork and knife.

Ingredients

  • Cheese
  • rice
  • lettuce
  • guacamole

Steps

  1. Place a burrito on a work surface.
  2. Place 1/4 - 1/3 cup of rice just below the centre.
  3. Top with 1/4 - 1/3 cup beef, then some lettuce, corn, black beans, tomato, a sprinkle of red onion, coriander and cheese.
  4. Fold up the bottom to cover the filling then fold the edges in.
